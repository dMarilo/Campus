Getting Started

Welcome to Campus

Campus is an all-in-one academic management platform designed for students, professors, and administrators. Use the sidebar to navigate between modules. Here's a quick look at what each section offers.

Module

Library

Browse the university's full catalogue of books, check availability, and manage borrowings all from a single, searchable table.

Browsing Books

Open Library from the sidebar to see every book in the system. Each row shows the title, author, publisher, edition, and how many copies are currently available.

  • Click on any book to open a detailed preview with the full description.
  • Available copy count updates in real time as borrowings are processed.
Borrowing Terminal

Physical borrowings are managed at /library/terminal — a dedicated kiosk-style interface for librarians. Students scan their ID, select a book copy by ISBN, and the system records the due date automatically.

  • Borrowing period and due dates are set by the system.
  • Overdue books are flagged in both the terminal and the student profile.
Admin tip: Use Add Book from the library page to register new titles. Each book can have multiple physical copies tracked by ISBN.
Module

Courses

Courses are the top-level academic units. Each course contains one or more classes (sections), which students enroll in for a specific semester.

Course Catalogue

The courses table lists all available courses with their code, name, and credit count. Click a course to open its detail page.

  • The course preview shows a full description and all active classes for that course.
  • Each class card links directly to the class detail page.
Creating Courses

Admins can add or edit courses using the Add Course button. Fill in the course name, code, description, and credit hours.

Module

Classes

A class is a concrete instance of a course — it has an assigned professor, a specific group of enrolled students, and a schedule of exams.

Enrolled Students

Open any class to see the full roster of enrolled students, their attendance records, and exam participation.

Exam Schedule

Each class has its own exam calendar. From the class page you can see all planned, open, and graded exams for that group.

  • Exam dates, times, and classrooms are listed at a glance.
  • Click an exam to see full results and statistics.
Module

Students

The students module gives administrators a complete picture of every enrolled student — personal info, academic history, borrowing records, and exam results.

Students Table

Browse and search all students. Filter by year of study or status (active, suspended, graduated). Click any row to open the full student profile.

Student Profile

The profile page is a comprehensive view broken into four sections:

  • Personal Information — name, DOB, gender.
  • Contact Information — email, phone, address.
  • Academic Information — index number, year, enrollment date.
  • Borrowing History — toggle between current and all-time borrowings. Overdue books are highlighted in red.
  • Exam Results — every exam the student has sat, their grade (out of 10), and whether they passed or failed.
The Passed / Failed counts in the Exam Results header give a quick summary without having to scroll through the table.
Module

Professors

View and manage the academic staff. Each professor profile shows their department, academic title, employment type, office, and current class assignments.

Professor Profile
  • Academic title and department are shown prominently.
  • Office location and office hours help students know when to reach out.
  • Employment type (full-time, part-time, visiting, etc.) is displayed in a readable format.
Module

Exams

The exams module is a global view of every assessment across all classes — midterms, finals, retakes, quizzes, oral exams, and lab exams.

Exam Types
  • Midterm — mid-semester assessment.
  • Final — end-of-semester comprehensive exam.
  • Retake — second attempt for students who didn't pass.
  • Quiz — short, focused assessment.
  • Oral — verbal examination.
  • Lab Exam — practical laboratory assessment.
Exam Lifecycle

Every exam moves through a clear set of statuses:

Planned Open Closed Graded

Exams can also be Canceled at any point before grading.

Results & Statistics

The exam detail page shows every student's grade, a pass rate percentage, and the class average. Grades are recorded on a 1 – 10 scale; the pass threshold is typically 6.

Your Account

Your Profile

Click your name in the sidebar or navigate to /profile to view and manage your personal account information.

What You'll See
  • Students — personal info, academic details (index, year, department, GPA), and a full exam results table showing every exam taken with grade and pass/fail status.
  • Professors — personal info, professional details (title, department, employment type, office).
  • Admins — account information and system access level.
Your profile data is pulled securely from the server using your login token — no sensitive data is stored in the browser beyond the session.
